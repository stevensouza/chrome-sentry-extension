Risk Scoring Reference

Understanding how extension security scores are calculated

What Your Security Score Means

80-100 Good Security: Your extensions have minimal security risks. Continue monitoring new installations.
50-79 Moderate Risk: Some extensions have elevated permissions. Review high-risk extensions and verify you trust their developers.
0-49 High Risk: Multiple high-risk extensions detected. Disable unnecessary extensions and only keep those from trusted sources.

How Your Score is Calculated

1
Individual Extension Risk Scores

Each extension receives a risk score (0-100) by adding up points from the Permission Risk Values table below. For example, an extension with "history" permission (+5) and "Developer Mode" install (+15) gets a risk score of 20.

2
Calculate Average Risk

We average the risk scores across all your extensions. If you have 8 extensions with risk scores of 50, 25, 15, 10, 5, 3, 2, and 0, the average risk is (50+25+15+10+5+3+2+0) ÷ 8 = 13.75

3
Your Overall Security Score

Security Score = 100 - Average Risk. Using the example above: 100 - 13.75 = 86.25 (displayed as 86). A higher score means better security!

Why this works: Even if you have one high-risk extension (50 points), several low-risk extensions will keep your overall score high. This reflects your overall browser security posture, not just the worst-case scenario.
